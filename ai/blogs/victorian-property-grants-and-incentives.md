# Victorian Property Grants & Incentives for 2025

If you’re a property investor looking to grow your portfolio, Victorian property grants and government-backed incentives in 2025 could help you save thousands — and open doors to new opportunities. With rising property prices and tighter lending conditions, making the most of available grants is more important than ever.

In this guide, we’ll walk you through the most relevant financial support programs available to investors in Victoria — and show you exactly how to use them to your advantage.

## Unlocking Victorian Property Grants: What You Need to Know

Government incentives aren’t just for first-home buyers. In fact, Victoria offers a variety of property grants and financial programs aimed at supporting regional development, sustainable building, and long-term property investment.

While not all programs involve direct cash payments, many provide significant cost savings through concessions, rebates, or reduced fees. Therefore, understanding these opportunities is key to a strong investment strategy.

## 1. First Home Owner Grant (FHOG) – A Step Into Property Investment

Although traditionally designed for owner-occupiers, the First Home Owner Grant (FHOG) can still benefit strategic investors. For example, if you plan to live in the property first — and then rent it out later — you may qualify for the $10,000 grant when purchasing a new home under $750,000.

This is particularly useful for investors using a rentvesting approach: buying in affordable growth areas while continuing to rent where they want to live.

## 2. Stamp Duty Savings Through Victorian Property Incentives

Stamp duty is often one of the largest upfront costs for investors. Fortunately, several Victorian property incentives can reduce or even eliminate this cost:

- Full exemption for eligible first-home buyers purchasing property under $600,000
- Partial concession for properties priced between $600,000 and $750,000
- Off-the-plan concession, which lowers the taxable amount based on the build status

As a result, investors can free up capital to improve the property or fund future purchases. In addition, those buying new builds or house-and-land packages may qualify for multiple benefits.

## 3. Land Tax Exemptions and Structuring for Investors

While not technically a grant, land tax has a major impact on your bottom line. The structure you use to purchase property — whether as an individual, company, or trust — determines how much tax you’ll pay each year.

For example, trusts and companies are often taxed at higher rates, but can offer asset protection or estate planning benefits. Therefore, speaking to a tax specialist is crucial before making your next purchase.

## 4. Regional Victorian Property Grants and Investment Opportunities

In recent years, the Victorian Government has pushed hard to drive growth in regional areas. As a result, investors have access to a range of support programs and local council incentives.

For example, areas such as Ballarat, Bendigo, and Geelong benefit from:

- Infrastructure funding
- Fast rail and road upgrades
- Increased demand for rentals and owner-occupiers

Additionally, lower property prices and higher rental yields make regional towns highly attractive for long-term returns. Therefore, investors seeking value should not overlook these high-growth zones.

## 5. Sustainability Rebates and Upgrades

Property improvements aren’t just cosmetic — they can also qualify for sustainability incentives. The Victorian Energy Upgrades (VEU) program offers generous rebates for eco-friendly upgrades, such as:

- Solar panels and hot water systems
- Efficient heating, cooling, and insulation
- LED lighting and smart home features

As a result, you reduce operating costs while increasing the property’s appeal to tenants. In addition, green upgrades help future-proof your portfolio against environmental regulation and rising utility prices.
